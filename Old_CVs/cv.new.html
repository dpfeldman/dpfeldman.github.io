RESEARCH ACCOMPLISHMENTS

Exact, Analytic Calculation of Excess Entropy, Statistical Complexity and the epsilon-Machine for Finite-Range, One-Dimensional Spin Systems. (With J. Crutchfield.) These quantities use information theory and the theory of formal computation to measure the memory and intrinsic computational ability of statistical mechanical systems. Carried out extensive comparison of information theoretic, computational mechanical and statistical mechanical approaches to the discovery and quantification of patterns in statistical systems.

Analysis of a Recently-Proposed Statistical Complexity Measure. (With J. Crutchfield.) Proved that the complexity measure suggested by Lopez-Ruiz, Mancini, and Calbet vanishes exponentially fast in system size for all finite-step ergodic Markov chains. Concluded that the proposed measure is unsuitable for use in a statistical mechanical context.

CURRENT RESEARCH

Extension of results to more than one dimension. (With J. Crutchfield.) Developing C++ programs to complement analytic techniques. Will use exact results for 1D systems to develop a theory of "finite size scaling" for epsilon-machines. Will then carry out calculations for cellular automata and the 2D Ising model.

Multifractal Spectrum. (With K. Young and J. Crutchfield.) Will compare the description of a system's fluctuations provided by the multifractal spectra to information and computation theoretic measures of structure. Will compare and contrast thermodynamic phase transitions with the "phase transitions" that arise in the thermodynamic formalism of dynamical systems.
