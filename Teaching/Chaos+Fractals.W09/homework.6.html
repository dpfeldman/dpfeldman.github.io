Homework Six


Due at the start of class, Monday February 2, 2009.

Explore the bifurcation diagram for the logistic equation. To do so, use one or more of the following java applets:

  • http://web.mst.edu/~vojtat/class_355/chapter1/logmap_bif.html
  • http://math.bu.edu/DYSYS/applets/OrbitDiagram.html

If you want, you may do this set of experiments with someone else in the class, and hand in only one write-up. I'm main point of this is to get some experience zooming in and out of the bifurcation diagram and thinking about what it means.

  1. Zoom in on different regions of the bifucation diagram and have fun.
  2. Use the bifurcation diagram to identify r values for which the orbits of the logistic equation have the following properties. There are many possible correct answers
    • Period 6 (Hint: look near period 3.)
    • Chaotic behavior for some r not equal to 4.
    • Period 5 (Hint: Look between 3.7 and 3.8.)
  3. At what value of r does the period 2 behavior split into period 4 behavior?
  4. At what value of r does the period 4 behavior split into period 8 behavior?
