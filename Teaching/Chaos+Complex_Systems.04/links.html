Chaos, Fractals, Nonlinear Dynamics

  • FAQ for sci.nonlinear. Lots of good, reliable information here. Might be a little on the technical side at times.

  • The Chaos Hypertextbook. A nice introduction to chaos and fractals, at a level slightly below that of this course.

  • Complexicon. This site, originally designed to accompany an exhibit at the explortorium science museum, is a very good glossary of some of the key terms in chaos and fractals. There are also brief biographies of many of the physicists and mathematicians who have done work in dynamical systems.


Cellular Automata

  • Exploring the Space of Cellular Automata. This site has a very easy-to-use interface that will let you produce tons of space-time diagrams. (To find this interfact, follow the elementray CA link or go here.
  • The Primordial Soup Kitchen. This site was built by David Griffeath, a math prof at the University of Wisconsin. Lots of colorful applets here.


Networks

  • Physics of the Web. An interesting, mostly non-technical article on the properties of the network formed by the www.


Fractals

  • The Cantor Set. Excellent info from Neil Carothers.
