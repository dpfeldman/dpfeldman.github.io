Has Feminism Changed Science? by Londa Schiebinger

Chapter 4: Clash of Cultures



Central Premises

  • The tension underlying the clashing cultures is that between the division private and public.
  • There are many aspects of scientific culture(s) that tend to alienate women.
  1. The Gendering of Science
    • A root of the clash of cultures is the notion of Sexual Complementarity: Women are not equals, but complementary opposites to men.
    • "The ideal virtues of femininity --- required for the joys of domestic life --- [are] portrayed [within the culture of science] as a personal failing of women."
  2. What's in an image?
    • Most images people see and people internalize of science and scientists are masculine, socially dysfunctional, and male.
    • Physical appearances are another realm in which these cultures clash.
    • "Women successful in science often assimilate or are assimilated to masculine codes of honor."
  3. Women in Professional Culture
    • "Men and women fall easily into expected and comfortable behaviors which may unintentionally perpetuate women's subordinate stature."
    • Gender asymmetries influence student evaluations.
  4. Competition, Science, and Sports
    • Science is very competitive and modeled after a sports team. This is particularly true in the U.S.
    • There is a widespread belief that science is pushed forward most efficiently by competition between labs and scientists.
