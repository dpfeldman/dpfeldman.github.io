The Nature and Language of Mathematics

Instructor: Jen Gutmann

The Nature and Language of Mathematics is an introductory course designed to help students discover the connections between mathematics and other areas of human understanding. By exploring diverse mathematics topics, students will see the varied roles that mathematics play in our world. Topics covered will include graph theory, probability, estimation, and linear equations. The majority of in-class work will take place in small groups, allowing students to be active, engaged learners. Students will read several articles, as well as "Fermat's Enigma", a popular book chronicling the recent solution of a 350 year old problem in number theory. Through this course, the student will be encouraged to understand the patterns, language, and logic that underlies what we call "mathematics".

Evaluation will be based on class participation and group work, weekly projects and assignments, occasional quizzes, and a research paper. Students may also be asked to present their research topic orally.

No prerequisites are required for this course. Lab fee: $10. QR.
