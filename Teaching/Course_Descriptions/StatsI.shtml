Introduction to Statistics and Research Design

Instructor: Sean Todd

Web page for the winter 2001 course

This course introduces the basics of statistical analysis that can be used in either a scientific or social science frame of reference. While this course teaches you to perform both nonparametric and simple parametric analysis both by hand and by computer, an emphasis will be placed on understanding the principles and assumptions of each test, rather than mathematical ability per se. We will also learn how to report statistical results in journal format, and there will be plenty of lab time to sharpen your skills. Evaluation is based on lab participation, two quizzes and several choices of take-home problems. Introductory/Intermediate. Prerequisite: A college mathematics course, or permission of the instructor (student should have a solid background in albegra.) QR.
