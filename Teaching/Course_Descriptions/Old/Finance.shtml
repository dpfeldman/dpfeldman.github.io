Microeconomics and Personal Finance Mathematics

Instructor: Visitor TBA


This course propsal is still quite preliminary. It has not been approved by academic affairs. Davis Taylor and I will continue to develop the course and will seek a visiting instructor to teach it. We hope to offer the course during the 1999-2000 academic year. We will return to academic affairs with a more detailed proposal in the spring of 1999.


Topics covered may include some or all of the following: computing interest rates, understanding economic indicators, elementary accounting, elementary statistics, exchange rates. This will be an introductory, hands-on course, emphasizing business applications of math, microeconomics, and the use of spreadsheet programs.

Evaluation will be based on criteria to be determined. Use of a spread sheet program will be required, but no computer experience is necessary.

Prerequisites: A good high school algebra course. Introductory. QR.
