Math for Human Ecologists

Instructor: Don Cass

This is not a remedial course. It is designed for students who have a reasonably solid background in algebra and who wish to gain more experience using math to describe the real world. Topics covered will include: making and interpreting graphs, logarithms and exponential growth, and elementary statistics. Students who have difficulty applying math to concrete problems may find this course helpful before taking biology, physics, chemistry, or economics courses. Math for Human Ecologists is not a prerequisite for these courses, however.

Math for Human Ecologists can also serve as a "pre-calculus" course for those wishing to hone their algebra skills before entering the calculus sequence. Students should be aware, however, that serving as a gateway to calculus is not the primary function of Math for Human Ecologists.
