Lab 1: Fermi Problems


Warm Up Exercises:

  1. Without using a calculator, convert the following to scientific notation:
    1. 24,000,000.
    2. 0.000070.
    3. Six Billion.
    4. Seven Hundred Trillion
    5. One one Thousandth.

  2. What is the order of magnitude of the above numbers?


The Fun Stuff

Each group should write up one solution to each problem. The write up can be quite informal. However, it should be legible and, most importantly, you should state your assumptions clearly.

  1. Estimate the surface area of all the leaves on all the trees on Mount Desert Island. Compare this with the surface area of MDI.

  2. You run a small non-profit that works to pressure small businesses to conduct themselves in an environmentally sound way. You have recently learned that both barbers and piano tuners in New York City are using a nasty chemical. The barbers use it to clean their scissors and the tuners use it on the metal piano wires. (This is completely fictitious.) Your organization has very limited resources and you can only afford to try to educate one group: barbers or piano tuners. Which should you try to contact? Make order of magnitude estimates of the number of barbers and the number of piano tuners in New York City.

  3. (Due to Eric Mazur.) On a Saturday afternoon, you pull into a parking lot with unmetered spaces near a shopping area. You circle around, but there are no empty spots. You decide to wait at one end of the lot where you can see (and command) about 20 spaces. How long do you have to wait before someone frees up a space?
