COMMUNITY-BASED RESEARCH

Science that Responds to Community Needs

Madeleine Scammell

The Loka Institute

Turrets I

Friday 14 May 1999

11:30 - 1:00


I will begin my presentation by giving a definition of community-based research (CBR). Briefly, CBR is research conducted by, for or with the participation of community members. CBR presents an alternative to the "top-down", "expert" driven research predominant in the U.S. I will then briefly give several examples of CBR, including work done in North Carolina on hog farming, and the over 35 "science shops" currently operating in the Netherlands.

I will then discuss guidelines for CBR recently proposed and implemented by communities and scientists across the country. Throughout the talk I will also discuss our role at the Loka Institute in helping establish CBR projects here and abroad as part of our community research network project. The Community research network is a transnational project linking hundreds of universities and grassroots organizations doing community based research.

This talk will be quite informal. There will be plenty of time for questions and discussion both during and after my presentation.
