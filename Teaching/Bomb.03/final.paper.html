Final Paper Guidelines


Here are some guidelines for your final paper.

  • Your paper should not be a research paper. You should draw heavily on the readings we've done, but there's no need to do additional research.
  • Your final paper should be synthetic and analytic: make connections, analyze, draw conclusions, take a position, stake out some territory.
  • Your paper should be around five to eight pages in length.
  • Before writing, have a clear idea of who your intended audience is.
  • I recommend writing in the first person.
  • Your paper should be typed or word-processed, and you should fully cite all sources. I don't care what format you use, but you should be consistent.
  • Feel free to use a format other than a standard essay. Possibilities include:
    • An open letter. The letter could be backward in time (e.g. to Oppenheimer in 1950), or current, or to someone in the future (perhaps to a scientist in 2050).
    • An op-ed piece or an opinion piece for a magazine or newspaper.
    • A work of fiction.
    If you plan on trying out any of these "non-standard" formats, please let me know beforehand.
