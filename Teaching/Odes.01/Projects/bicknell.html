Should I walk or should I run?: an investigation into how pace will effect the wetness of a person in a rainstorm.

Jericho Bicknell


When caught in the rain many people have a tendency to run quickly to get undercover. Is this really the way to stay dry? If you walk, you will be soaked by the rain that is hitting the top of your head; if you run, will you not also be hit by the rain that you yourself run into? The purpose of this project is to present a number of different mathematical models in poster form that try to answer this question, taking into account different variables such as the shape of the person, and the angle at which the rain is coming at the poor runner/walker.
