Organization of technology discussion with faculty:

  • Use the first half of next faculty meeting to get information like:
    • what do faculty members use now in the way of technology in the classroom?
    • what do faculty members need to make their jobs better?
    • do they see a large difference between students concerning technological knowledge/use?
    • How do they want to serve student needs? Focus on those with minimal technological knowledge (by creating a non-dedicated computer/ teaching space) or increase ability of highly skilled students?
    • What would they like to do with a class, assuming they're limited by the present level of classroom technology?

When asking these questions, we need some way of organizing the comments and seeing how they rank when compared to eachother; writing on a tablet and having individuals put stickers next to ones they like was one suggestion. Faculty will need time for the "digestion" of ideas, and we should allow for (and encourage?) inter- and intra-resource area discussion.

Concerning the 4th computer services position, we should ask what else could be done with the extra person?

IMPORTANT!! Call a meeting for staff as well as faculty concerning these technological issues. Treat them as importantly as faculty are -- see their needs, address traditional inequalities between faculty and staff.
