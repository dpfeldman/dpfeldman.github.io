What do you need to help you do your job (interdisciplinary teaching and research) better?

Below are all the responses we received to this question. The items are listed in the order in which they were given by faculty.

  • a space to teach and use computers both
  • video equipment
  • a person to meet the technical needs
  • office computers comparable to campus/lab computers
  • access to a color printer
  • a video team to archive public events at the college and tape public records
  • reliable internet access from home
  • reliable photocopiers
  • programing for the satellite we have, a dish network
  • space for viewing TV
  • access to use the weather station
  • opaque projector
  • LCD panels for projectors, to project computer screens
  • computers in a private area of the language area for practicing languages
  • the ability to catalog/digitize slide collections
  • someone to organize the image libraries
  • laser technology * a plan for transformation from VHS to DVD
  • more money for the film collection for the library
  • a multimedia cart to roll between classrooms
  • communication and computer equipment for the islands and boat
  • two permanent slide projectors to always be available in the same place
  • turning forks, spades, edgers, and a replacement plan for basic equipment
  • more workshops on the software used for data representation in the classroom
  • scanning equipment for the library
  • someone to take care of all the equipment
  • a reliable source of transportation (car) for students* use
  • every classroom outfitted like the Library Seminar room
  • soundproof space
  • decent blackboards/whiteboards
  • new projector in the lecture hall
  • microscopes in the biology lab
  • better lighting in the classrooms; floor lamps
  • cameras and enlargers
  • slides: the ability to buy, make and replace them
  • a 3-year purchase plan that integrates all resource areas
  • space and equipment for a student radio station
  • submarine
